# Peter Lynch Fundamental Filter

## Core Metrics

| Metric | Threshold | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| EPS growth | > 20% YoY | Consistent preferred over one-off |
| PEG ratio | < 1.0 preferred | P/E ÷ EPS growth rate. <1 = undervalued growth |
| ROE | > 15% | Capital efficiency |
| Debt/Equity | < 0.5 | Clean balance sheet preferred |
| Promoter holding | > 50% | Skin in the game |
| Institutional ownership | Rising quarter on quarter | Smart money accumulating |

## The Lynch Story Test
**Every stock must have a one-sentence thesis that a 12-year-old can understand.**

Good examples:
- "SEDEMAC makes engines for small ICE vehicles and is ramping e2W exports"
- "NETWEB builds HPC servers and has more orders than last year's revenue"
- "EMMVEE makes solar panels and just got ALMM List-II approval"

If you can't state the thesis in one sentence, you don't understand the business well enough to hold it through volatility.

## Lynch Categories (for Indian Markets)
- **Slow growers:** Dividend-paying PSUs, utilities — not targets
- **Stalwarts:** Large FMCG, private banks, IT services — defensive holds
- **Fast growers:** Small/mid-cap with 20%+ EPS growth — primary targets
- **Cyclicals:** Capital goods, metals, chemicals — buy at cycle bottom
- **Turnarounds:** Debt-laden cos restructuring — special situations
- **Asset plays:** Land, brand value, hidden balance sheet — rare

## Verdict Classification
| Score | Verdict |
|-------|---------|
| PEG <1, EPS >25%, ROE >20%, clean debt | Strong Lynch |
| PEG 1–1.5, EPS 15–25%, moderate debt | Acceptable |
| PEG >1.5, EPS <15%, or deteriorating | Weak — downgrade setup |
