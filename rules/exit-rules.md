# Exit Rules

Five permanent rules derived from real trades where violations cost measurable rupees. These are not guidelines — they are hard stops.

## The 5 Rules

### Rule 1 — Trailing Stop: 18–20% Below Swing High
Set the trailing stop at 18–20% below the most recent swing high. Update it weekly (only upward, never lower). Never remove a trailing stop once set.

### Rule 2 — 3-Tranche Exit: Never Fully Exit on Emotion
- 33% at T1
- 33% at T2
- Trail final 34% with trailing stop

No full exit on emotion. No single-tranche panic exits.

### Rule 3 — Thesis Invalidation Only
If the original buy reason (the thesis) is still intact, do not exit on price alone. Define the thesis invalidation trigger in one sentence before entry. If that trigger has not fired, hold.

### Rule 4 — Limit Orders Only (Market Orders Banned for Exits)
All exits via limit orders placed 2–3% above current bid. Market orders after intraday drops fill at the worst intraday price. Market orders for exits are permanently banned.

### Rule 5 — Act on Sector Macro Calls Fully and Quickly
When a sector headwind is identified early (before price confirms it), exit the entire sector exposure immediately. Do not wait for price confirmation. This is the proven edge (see Infosys/Birlasoft template below).

## Trade Autopsies — The Source of These Rules

### 🔴 MISTAKE #1 — MTAR Technologies (Feb 9, 2026)
**What happened:** Sold ₹3,166 during a clean stage-2 uptrend on a 2-day, 3.5% pullback. Stock hit ₹8,449 (+167%) within 3.5 months. **Cost: ₹8.72L in missed gains.**
> **MTAR WARNING:** In a stage-2 uptrend (higher highs + higher lows), a 2–5 day pullback of <5% is noise. Only exit on a CLOSE below the 20-day EMA or breakdown of the prior swing low on volume.

### 🔴 MISTAKE #2 — Laurus Labs (Dec 15, 2025)
**What happened:** Sold ₹999 at the ₹1,000 round-number level. Stock climbed to ₹1,457 (+46%). **Cost: ₹1.26L.**
> **Round Number Rule:** Round numbers (₹100, ₹500, ₹1,000, ₹5,000) are SUPPORT, not sell signals. A real breakdown requires a CLOSE below support + above-average sell volume.

### 🔴 MISTAKE #3 — Ather Energy (Dec 3, 2025)
**What happened:** Sold ₹689 after a 10% pullback. EV thesis completely intact. Stock hit ₹1,069 (+55%). **Cost: ₹74.8K.**
> **10% Pullback Rule:** A 10–15% pullback in a growth/thematic stock with intact thesis = normal volatility, not an exit signal. Define thesis invalidation before entry, not at the moment of discomfort.

### 🔴 MISTAKE #4 — Aurobindo Pharma (Apr 10, 2026)
**What happened:** Sold 360 shares at ₹1,349 on the exact day a 5-month base was breaking out. Peak: ₹1,550. **Cost: ₹72.4K.**
> **Base Breakout Rule:** Never sell on the day a base is breaking out. Use a trailing stop (8–10%) instead of a full exit.

### 🔴 MISTAKE #5 — Netweb Technologies (Jun 8, 2026)
**What happened:** Market order exit after intraday drop from ₹4,934 → filled near ₹4,115. Intraday high was ₹4,569 — 10% above fill. **Cost: ₹83.9K vs a limit order.**
> **Market Order Ban:** Never use market orders for exits after a sharp intraday decline. Always use limit orders.

### ✅ WIN PATTERN — Infosys + Birlasoft (Jan 2026)
**What happened:** Both exited correctly before the IT sector crashed. **Capital protected: ₹1.53L combined.**
> **Replicate:** (1) Identify sector headwind early. (2) Exit fully when headwind confirms — don't wait for price. (3) This is the proven edge. Apply to every position in a deteriorating sector.
